How To Price Freelance Projects

Published June 29, 2026 · By SoloForge

Hourly vs Project Pricing: The Real Answer

Hourly pricing is safer for you. Project pricing is better for the client. The solution: estimate your hours, multiply by your rate, add 15% buffer, quote the project price. Client gets certainty. You get paid for your actual effort plus a safety margin.

The 15% Buffer Rule

Every project takes longer than you think. Always. The 15% buffer covers: scope creep, unexpected complexity, revisions, communication overhead. If you finish early, refund the buffer and you have a client for life. If not, you do not lose money.

Three Pricing Tiers That Always Work

Offer three options: Basic (the minimum), Standard (best value, 50% more than Basic), Premium (2x Basic, includes extras). Most clients pick Standard. Basic exists to make Standard look reasonable. Premium exists for clients who want everything.

When to Raise Your Rates

Raise your rates when: you have more work than you can handle, clients stop pushing back on price, or you have not raised them in 12 months. Raise by 15-25% each time. Existing clients keep their rate for 3 months as a courtesy. New clients get the new rate immediately.
