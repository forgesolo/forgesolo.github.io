How To Start Freelancing

Published June 17, 2026 · By SoloForge

The First Step Is Smaller Than You Think

You do not need a website, a brand, or a portfolio of 20 projects. You need one skill that someone will pay for, and the willingness to reach out to 50 people. Most aspiring freelancers never send the first pitch. Do that, and you are already ahead of 95% of people.

Pick Your First Offer

Do not be a "general freelancer." Pick one specific thing: I design landing pages. I write email sequences. I build Excel dashboards. Specific beats broad every time. Clients hire specialists, not generalists.

Find Your First Client (This Week)

Go to Twitter/X. Search "looking for [your skill]." Reply to 10 posts with a specific suggestion, not a sales pitch. Do this every day for a week. Someone will ask about your rates. That is your first lead.

Set Up Your Financial System Early

Before your first client pays, set up: a pricing formula, an invoice template, an income tracker, and a separate bank account. The freelancers who fail are not the ones without skills ? they are the ones without systems.

Start With Free Tools, Upgrade When You Need
