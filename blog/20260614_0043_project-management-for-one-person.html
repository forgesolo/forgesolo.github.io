Project Management For One Person

Published June 14, 2026 · By SoloForge

You Are Not a Company. Stop Managing Like One.

One-person project management is fundamentally different from team project management. You do not need sprints, standups, or velocity tracking. You need to know three things: what is due next, how much it pays, and how long it takes.

The Single-View Dashboard

All your active projects should fit on one screen. Project name, client, deadline, status, budget, hours spent. If you need to scroll, you are tracking too much detail. Simplify until it fits.

Statuses That Actually Help

Four statuses are enough: Not Started, In Progress, Waiting on Client, Done. Anything more granular is noise. "Waiting on Client" is the most important ? it tells you exactly where your bottlenecks are.

Batch Similar Work

Switch costs are real. Do all your proposals on Monday morning. All client emails on Wednesday. All creative work in long blocks. Context switching kills solo productivity more than anything else.
