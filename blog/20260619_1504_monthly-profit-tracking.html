Monthly Profit Tracking

Published June 19, 2026 · By SoloForge

Revenue Is Vanity. Profit Is Sanity.

Many freelancers celebrate their $8,000 month without realizing they spent $3,500 on tools, ads, subcontractors, and taxes. Revenue tells a story. Profit tells the truth.

The Simple P&L for Solo Makers

You need exactly two lists: what came in, and what went out. That is it. List every revenue source (freelance, products, affiliate, consulting, other). List every expense (software, marketing, fees, taxes). Subtract. That number is the only one that matters.

Why Monthly Tracking Beats Yearly

Yearly accounting is for tax season. Monthly tracking is for decision making. When you see your profit monthly, you notice trends: December is always slow (plan for it), March always spikes (lean in), software costs creep up every quarter (audit your subscriptions).

Save 25% for Taxes Automatically

Every month, move 25% of profit to a separate savings account. Do not touch it. When tax season comes, you have the money. The freelancers who get into trouble are the ones who spend 100% of what they earn and then owe thousands with nothing saved.
