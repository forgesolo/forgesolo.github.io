Why Most Freelancers Undercharge

If you are setting your rates based on what "feels right" or what other freelancers charge, you are almost certainly undercharging. The truth is, your rate should be calculated from your costs and goals ? not from market averages.

The Simple Formula

Your minimum hourly rate = (Monthly Costs + Income Goal) / Billable Hours. That is it. Three numbers. Most freelancers spend years guessing when they could have figured it out in 30 seconds.

Step 1: Know Your Real Costs

List everything: rent, software subscriptions (Notion, Figma, Adobe, GitHub Copilot), internet, phone, insurance, marketing. Freelancers typically underestimate costs by 30%. Write them all down. Right now.

Step 2: Set a Real Income Goal

Do not think "what can I survive on." Think "what do I want to earn." $5,000/month is a common starting goal. That is $60,000/year ? roughly the median salary for a skilled professional in most countries.

Step 3: Be Honest About Hours

You cannot bill 40 hours per week. Realistic billable hours for freelancers are 60-80 per month. The rest is admin, marketing, learning, email, proposals. If you assume 100 billable hours at $5,000 goal and $1,000 costs, your minimum is $60/hour. Charge less and you are losing money.
