The Solo Business Stack

Running a one-person business requires different tools than a company. You need speed, simplicity, and low cost. Here are the tools that actually matter for solo makers in 2026.

Project Management: Keep It Simple

You do not need Jira or Monday.com. A simple spreadsheet or Notion page works better for one person. Track: project name, client, deadline, budget, hours spent. That is it. Five columns. Anything more is procrastination disguised as organization.

Finance: Know Your Numbers

The number one reason freelancers fail: they do not know their numbers. You need three things: a pricing calculator, an income tracker, and a monthly profit dashboard. That is exactly what the Solo Income Toolkit provides.

Client Communication: Templates Save Hours

You write the same emails over and over: cold pitches, follow-ups, proposals, rate increase notices, testimonial requests. Each one takes 20 minutes to write from scratch. A template takes 2 minutes to customize. Over a year, that is 50+ hours saved.

Marketing: Free Tools Are Your Best Ads

The most effective marketing for solo makers is a free tool. A calculator, a checklist, a template. It attracts exactly the right people, demonstrates your value, and converts naturally. No ads, no spam, no social media addiction.
